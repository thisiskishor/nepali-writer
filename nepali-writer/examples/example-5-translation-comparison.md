# Example 5: Translation Comparison

Literal (word-by-word) translations vs. natural Nepali — why they differ.

---

## Key Principle

**Never translate word-for-word.** Understanding *why* literal translation fails teaches the core of Nepali translation.

---

## Comparison 1: Emotions & Sensations (Dative Construction)

### English
"I am hungry" / "I feel happy"

### ❌ Literal Translation (Wrong)
```
म भोकाएको छु ।
(I am hungry — nominative construction)

म खुसी छु ।
(I am happy — nominative construction)
```

### ✓ Natural Nepali (Correct)
```
मलाई भोक लाग्यो ।
(Hunger touched me — dative construction)

मलाई खुशी लाग्यो ।
(Happiness touched me — dative construction)
```

### Why the Difference?

| Aspect | Literal (Wrong) | Natural (Correct) |
|--------|---|---|
| **Grammar** | Nominative: म (I) + verb | Dative: मलाई (to me) + verb |
| **Meaning** | "I am the state of hunger" | "Hunger comes to me / I experience hunger" |
| **Sound** | Awkward, non-native | Natural Nepali idiom |
| **Verb** | Static (छु = am) | Action (लाग्यो = touched/came) |

### Cultural Note

Nepali treats emotions/sensations as external events that "happen to" the person, not states the person "is in". This affects grammar choice fundamentally.

## Comparison 2: Weather Verbs (Subject Omission)

### English
"It is raining" / "It is cold"

### ❌ Literal Translation (Wrong)
```
यो पानी पर्दैछ ।
(It water is-falling — overly literal with pronoun)

यो चिसो छ ।
(It cold is — noun used as adjective)
```

### ✓ Natural Nepali (Correct)
```
पानी पर्दैछ ।
(Rain is-falling — subject implied, no need for "it")

चिसो लाग्यो ।
(Cold-ness touched [me] — experience rather than state)
```

### Why the Difference?

In Nepali:
- Weather verbs don't need explicit "it" subject (unlike English)
- "Cold" is experienced, not a property of weather
- Subject is implied from context

---

## Comparison 3: Incorrect Verb Choice

### English
"Please sit down"

### ❌ Literal Translation (Wrong)
```
कृपया सिट गर्नुहोस् ।
(Please sit do — transliterated verb)
```

### ✓ Natural Nepali (Correct)
```
कृपया बस्नुहोस् ।
(Please sit — correct verb बस्नु for sitting)
```

### Why the Difference?

| Aspect | Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| **Verb** | सिट गर्नु (sit do — calqued from English "do sit") | बस्नु (Nepali verb for "sit") |
| **Construction** | Mixing "do" with English noun | Pure Nepali verb |
| **Sound** | Artificial, not native | Natural Nepali |

**Other verb examples:**
- "Stand up" → खडा हुनुहोस् (not "stand do")
- "Lie down" → सुत्नुहोस् (not "lie do")

---

## Comparison 4: Greeting Formulas

### English
"Good morning"

### ❌ Literal Translation (Wrong)
```
गुड मर्निङ् लेख्नुहोस् ।
(transliterated English phrase)
```

### ✓ Natural Nepali (Correct)
```
शुभ प्रभात ।
(Auspicious morning — traditional greeting)

सुप्रभात ।
(Good morning — more informal/literary)
```

### Why the Difference?

| Aspect | Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| **Approach** | Direct transliteration | Traditional Nepali greeting formula |
| **Cultural fit** | Foreign | Culturally appropriate |
| **Naturalness** | Never used in Nepali | Recognized greeting |

**Other greetings:**
- "Good evening" → शुभ साँझ / शुभ सन्ध्या
- "Good night" → शुभ रात्रि / शुभ निद्रा
- "Welcome" → स्वागत छ / स्वागतम्

## Comparison 5: Idioms and Expressions

### English
"I understand"

### ❌ Literal Translation (Wrong)
```
म अन्डरस्ट्यान्ड गर्छु ।
(I understand do — transliterated English)
```

### ✓ Natural Nepali (Correct)
```
मलाई बुझियो ।
(Understanding came to me — dative again)
```

### Why?

Understanding is experienced (dative) not performed:
- Dative: मलाई (to me) + बुझियो (understood) = "I got understanding"
- Wrong nominative approach misses the idiom

**Other expressions:**
| English | Literal (Wrong) | Natural (Correct) |
|---------|---|---|
| "Thank you" | धन्यवाद ग्राह गर्छु | हार्दिक धन्यवाद |
| "I'm sorry" | मलाई माफ गर् | मलाई माफ गर्नुहोस् / मलाई गलती भयो |
| "You're right" | तपाईं सही हुनुहुन्छ | तपाईंको कुरा ठिक छ |

## Core Translation Principles

### 1. Word Order (SOV not SVO)
- ❌ "I rice eat" (English word order)
- ✓ "म भात खान्छु" (Nepali: I rice eat)

### 2. Grammar Category (Dative for Emotions)
- ❌ "म खुसी छु" (I happy am)
- ✓ "मलाई खुशी लाग्यो" (happiness touched me)

### 3. Verb Selection (Native words)
- ❌ "सिट गर्नु" (sit do)
- ✓ "बस्नु" (Nepali verb)

### 4. Idiom Adaptation (Not literal)
- ❌ "गुड मर्निङ्"
- ✓ "शुभ प्रभात"

### 5. Subject Omission (Implied subjects)
- ❌ "यो पानी पर्दैछ" (this water falls)
- ✓ "पानी पर्दैछ" (rain falls — it is implied)

---

## Quick Reference: Wrong vs. Right Patterns

| Pattern | ❌ Wrong | ✓ Right | Lesson |
|---------|---------|---------|--------|
| Emotion | म खुसी छु | मलाई खुशी लाग्यो | Use dative |
| Weather | यो चिसो छ | चिसो लाग्यो | Omit subject |
| Action verb | सिट गर्नु | बस्नु | Use native verb |
| Greeting | गुड मर्निङ् | शुभ प्रभात | Adapt idiom |
| Word order | म खान्छु भात | म भात खान्छु | Use SOV |

---

## When to Apply These Principles

✓ Translate for Nepali speakers (prioritize naturalness)  
✓ Technical or formal documents (clarity without awkwardness)  
✓ Marketing copy (cultural resonance)  
✓ Social media (authentic voice)
