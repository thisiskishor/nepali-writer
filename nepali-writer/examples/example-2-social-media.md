# Example 2: Social Media Content

English → Nepali translation for Instagram/social media marketing.

---

## English (Input)

```
🎉 Just launched our new coffee shop in Kathmandu! 
Come visit us and enjoy freshly brewed coffee and homemade pastries. 
Tag a friend you'd like to bring with you! ☕✨
#CoffeeLovers #KathmanduCafe #NewOpening
```

---

## Nepali (Output)

```
🎉 काठमाडौँमा हाम्रो नयाँ कफी शप खुलियो! 
आउनुहोस् र ताजा काढिएको कफी र घरमै बनाइएको पेस्ट्री खान आनन्द गर्नुहोस् ☕✨
आफ्नो साथीलाई ट्याग गरेर साथै लेखाइदिनुस्! 
#कफीप्रेमीहरू #काठमाडौँकफीशप #नयाँउद्घाटन
```

---

## Translation Notes

### Register & Tone
- **Register:** Informal/Conversational (friendly, engaging)
- **Tone:** Enthusiastic, inviting, brand voice
- **Audience:** Young urban Nepali speakers, social media savvy

### Key Translation Decisions

| English | Nepali | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| "Just launched" | खुलियो | Simple past (natural for announcements) |
| "Come visit us" | आउनुहोस् | Formal imperative (warm in social media context) |
| "Enjoy" | आनन्द गर्नुहोस् | More elegant than "enjoy" literal; conveys pleasure |
| "Freshly brewed" | ताजा काढिएको | Passive construction (coffee that was freshly drawn) |
| "Homemade" | घरमै बनाइएको | "Made at home" — emphasizes craft/authenticity |
| "Tag a friend" | ट्याग गरेर साथै लेखाइदिनुस् | "Tag and mention along" — request to share |
| Hashtags | #कफीप्रेमीहरू #काठमाडौँकफीशप #नयाँउद्घाटन | Partially translated (कफीप्रेमीहरू = coffee lovers, नयाँउद्घाटन = new opening) |

### Grammar Points Demonstrated

1. **Informal Register:** Conversational vocabulary and imperative forms
2. **Acceptable Code-Mixing:** कफी (coffee), पेस्ट्री (pastry), ट्याग (tag) — common in Nepali social media
3. **Imperative Tone:** आउनुहोस्, आनन्द गर्नुहोस्, लेखाइदिनुस् — action-oriented verbs
4. **Passive Constructions:** काढिएको (brewed), बनाइएको (made) — add craft/quality feeling
5. **Emoji Integration:** Emojis work across languages; placed naturally in text

### Vocabulary Choices

- **शप** (shop) — Accepted transliteration; also: दोकान (traditional)
- **कफी** (coffee) — Loanword; standard in modern Nepali
- **पेस्ट्री** (pastry) — Loanword; no direct Nepali equivalent
- **ट्याग** (tag) — Social media terminology; transliterated
- **आनन्द** (joy/pleasure) — Elevates "enjoy" to more poetic level

### Hashtag Strategy

Partially translating hashtags (#कफीप्रेमीहरू) makes content discoverable to Nepali speakers while maintaining social media hashtag conventions. Mix of Nepali and English hashtags is acceptable in bilingual contexts.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid (in this context)

❌ **Wrong:** काठमाडौँमा हाम्रो नयाँ कफी शप अब उपलब्ध छ। (too formal)
✓ **Correct:** काठमाडौँमा हाम्रो नयाँ कफी शप खुलियो! (conversational, punchy)

❌ **Wrong:** तपाईं आउनुहोस् (inconsistent formality — too stiff for social media)
✓ **Correct:** आउनुहोस् (direct invitation, warmer)

❌ **Wrong:** तपाईंको साथीलाई ट्याग गरनुहोस् (too formal for social context)
✓ **Correct:** आफ्नो साथीलाई ट्याग गरेर साथै लेखाइदिनुस् (conversational, action-oriented)

❌ **Wrong:** पकाइएको पेस्ट्री (technically correct but clunky)
✓ **Correct:** घरमै बनाइएको पेस्ट्री (more natural, emphasizes homemade quality)

---

## When to Use This Translation Style

- Social media posts (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
- Brand marketing and promotional content
- Community announcements
- Casual brand communication
- Engaging Nepali-speaking audiences online
