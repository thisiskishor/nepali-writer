## When to Reference Each Example

### Example 1: Formal Business Letter
**Use when:** Writing professional correspondence, business proposals, government requests, official documents
**Key lessons:**
- Formal register matching (तपाईं + गर्नुहुन्छ forms)
- SOV sentence structure in formal context
- Proper salutation (आदरणीय) and closing (भवदीय)
- Formal vocabulary selection

---

### Example 2: Social Media Content
**Use when:** Marketing content, brand voice, conversational Nepali, hashtags
**Key lessons:**
- Informal/friendly register
- Acceptable code-mixing (modern loanwords)
- Tone preservation across languages
- Cultural relevance and engagement

---

### Example 3: Technical Documentation
**Use when:** Software docs, system requirements, technical specifications
**Key lessons:**
- Domain-specific terminology
- Technical term translation/transliteration choices
- Clear, instructional tone
- Consistency in technical vocabulary

---

### Example 4: Grammar Corrections
**Use when:** Identifying and fixing common errors, understanding rules
**Key lessons:**
- Honorific matching (pronoun + verb consistency)
- Raswa/Dirgha vowel length distinction
- Padyog/Padbiyog (word joining) conventions
- Danda (।) punctuation vs. Latin period

---

### Example 5: Translation Comparison
**Use when:** Understanding why literal translation fails, learning natural Nepali idioms
**Key lessons:**
- Idiom adaptation (not word-for-word mapping)
- Dative construction for emotions/sensations
- Subject-implied weather verbs
- Register-appropriate greetings

---

### Example 6: Register Variations
**Use when:** Understanding honorific system and tone adjustments
**Key lessons:**
- Three-level honorific system (तँ, तिमी, तपाईं)
- Register-appropriate vocabulary
- Formal/semi-formal/informal contexts
- Relationship-based register selection
