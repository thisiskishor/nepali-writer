## Worked Examples

### Example 1: English → Nepali Translation (Formal Business Letter)

**Input (English):**
> Dear Mr. Sharma,
> I am writing to request an extension on the deadline for the project submission. Due to unforeseen circumstances, our team needs an additional two weeks to complete the work to your satisfaction.
> We assure you of our continued commitment to quality and timely delivery. Please advise if this arrangement is acceptable.
> Sincerely,
> Rajesh Thapa

**Output (Nepali):**
```
आदरणीय श्रीमान् शर्माजी,

उपर्युक्त विषयमा यस कार्यालयमा निवेदन दिनु परेकाले सादर निवेदन गर्दछु कि प्रकल्प
प्रस्तुत गर्ने समयसीमा अलिकति बढाइपाऊँ भनी विनती गर्दैछु। अप्रत्याशित परिस्थितिले गर्दा
हाम्रो टोलीलाई काम सन्तोषजनक रूपमा सम्पन्न गर्न थप दुई हप्ता आवश्यक भएको छ।

हामी गुणस्तर र समयमै डेलिभरीको प्रति आफ्नो प्रतिबद्धता निरन्तर रहेको कुरामा आश्वस्त गर्दछौं।
यस व्यवस्था स्वीकार्य छ वा छैन भनी कृपया सूचित गर्नुहोस्।

भवदीय,
राजेश थापा
```

**Translation notes:**
- Formal honorific (तपाईं + गर्नुहुन्छ) maintained throughout
- SOV structure preserved (subject-object-verb)
- Formal Nepali vocabulary (कार्यालय instead of "अफिस", प्रकल्प instead of "प्रोजेक्ट")
- Proper salutation (आदरणीय) and closing (भवदीय)
- Danda (।) used at sentence endings, not periods

### Example 4: Proofreading Example (Finding & Fixing Common Errors)

**Submitted Nepali text:**
```
❌ दिदि यहाँ बस् । (informal with formal pronoun mismatch)
❌ मैले घर मा जान्छु । (ergative case with intransitive verb + space in postposition)
❌ यो राम्रो छ. (Latin period instead of danda)
❌ गर्नु हुन्छ (split honorific compound)
```

**Corrected:**
```
✓ दिदी यहाँ बस्नुहोस् । (formal pronoun with formal verb)
  OR
✓ तिमी यहाँ बस । (informal pronoun with informal verb)

✓ म घरमा जान्छु । (nominative case for intransitive verb जानु + postposition attached)

✓ यो राम्रो छ । (danda used)

✓ गर्नुहुन्छ (honorific compound as one word)
```

## Part 1: Foundational Knowledge

### 1.1 Script & Writing System

Nepali is written in **Devanagari script** (देवनागरी). It is a syllabic script where:
- The basic unit is a consonant + vowel (CV) syllable.
- Consonant clusters are written as **conjunct characters** (half-forms).
- Vowels appear as independent letters (word-initial) or as **dependent vowel signs** attached to consonants.
- The inherent vowel /a/ is assumed after every consonant unless cancelled by a **हलन्त (्)**.

**Unicode punctuation note:** Always use the Devanagari **danda (।)** — Unicode U+0964 — as the full stop. Never use the Latin period (.) to end a Nepali sentence.

---

### 1.2 Sentence Structure: SOV (Subject-Object-Verb)

Nepali follows **Subject → Object → Verb** order. This is the single most important structural difference from English (SVO).

| English (SVO) | Nepali (SOV) |
|---------------|--------------|
| I eat rice. | म भात खान्छु । |
| She reads the book. | उनी किताब पढ्छन् । |
| We went to Kathmandu. | हामी काठमाडौँ गयौं । |

**Rules:**
- The **verb always comes last** in a simple sentence.
- **Adjectives precede** the noun they modify: राम्रो घर (beautiful house), ठूलो मान्छे (big person).
- **Adverbs of time** go at the start: आज म स्कुल गएँ । (Today I went to school.)
- **Postpositions** follow the noun (unlike English prepositions): घरमा (in the house), बजारबाट (from the market).

---

### 1.3 Honorific System (आदरार्थी प्रणाली) — CRITICAL

The honorific system is the most important distinction in Nepali. Every verb and second/third-person pronoun must match the social register.

**Three levels:**

| Level | Pronoun (2nd person) | Verb example (to go) | When to use |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| Low (निम्न) | तँ (ta) | जान्छस् (jaanchhas) | Very close friends, small children, pets |
| Middle (मध्यम) | तिमी (timi) | जान्छौ (jaanchhou) | Peers, siblings, acquaintances |
| High/Formal (उच्च) | तपाईं / हजुर | जानुहुन्छ (jaanuhunchha) | Elders, officials, unknown adults, formal contexts |

**Formal verb compound structure:**
- High honorific is formed by: **verb root + नु + हुन्छ/हुनुहुन्छ/भयो** etc.
- Example: गर्नु (to do):
  - Low/Middle present: गर्छ / गर्छौ
  - High present: गर्नुहुन्छ (garnuhunchha)
  - High past: गर्नुभयो (garnubhayo)
  - High imperative: गर्नुहोस् (garnus)

**CRITICAL RULE — Padyog:** Honorific compounds like गर्नुहुन्छ, खानुहुन्छ, जानुभयो are written as **one word**, not split apart.

---

### 1.4 Raswa (ह्रस्व) and Dirgha (दीर्घ) — Vowel Length Rules

This is the most common **spelling error** in AI-generated Nepali. Short forms (ि, ु) and long forms (ी, ू) are phonemically distinct.

**USE DIRGHA (long ी / ू) for:**
- Feminine adjectives ending in –ī: काली, गोरी, राम्री
- Feminine verbs: लेखी (lekhī)
- Numbers ending in –sī: अठासी (88), उनान्सी (99)
- Nouns ending in –ī: गाडी, कार्की, खेलाडी

**USE RASWA (short ि / ु) for:**
- Most adverbs/particles: माथि (above), मुनि (below), पछि (after), भोलि (tomorrow)
- Sanskrit prefix words: नि– (निवास), वि– (विदेश)

**Confusable pairs:**

| Incorrect | Correct | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| दिदि | दिदी | elder sister |
| पानि | पानी | water |
| गाडि | गाडी | vehicle |

---

### 1.5 Postpositions & Case Markers

Nepali uses postpositions (after the noun) instead of prepositions. They also mark grammatical case.

| Postposition | Name | English Equivalent | Example |
|--------------|------|-------------------|---------|
| ले | Ergative/Instrumental | by/with | मैले (I [subject of transitive verb]) |
| लाई | Dative/Accusative | to/for | मलाई (to me) |
| को/का/की | Genitive | 's / of | रामको किताब (Ram's book) |
| मा | Locative | in/at/on | घरमा (in the house) |
| बाट/देखि | Ablative | from | बजारबाट (from the market) |
| सँग/साथ | Comitative | with | साथीसँग (with a friend) |
| सम्म | Terminative | up to/until | काठमाडौँसम्म (up to Kathmandu) |

**CRITICAL:** Postpositions attach directly to nouns — no space between noun and postposition.
- ✓ घरमा (in the house)
- ✗ घर मा (WRONG)

---

### 1.6 Common Verb Forms (Present, Past, Future, Imperative)

**Present Tense (गर्नु — to do):**

| Subject | Form | Transliteration |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| म (I) | गर्छु | garchhu |
| तिमी (you – mid) | गर्छौ | garchhou |
| तपाईं (you – formal) | गर्नुहुन्छ | garnuhunchha |
| ऊ/उनी (he/she) | गर्छ/गर्छन् | garchha/garchhan |
| हामी (we) | गर्छौं | garchhaun |

**Simple Past Tense:**

| Subject | Form | Transliteration |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| मैले (I) | गरेँ | garen |
| तिमीले (you – mid) | गर्यौ | garyau |
| तपाईंले (you – formal) | गर्नुभयो | garnubhayo |
| उसले/उनले (he/she) | गर्यो/गरे | garyo/gare |

**Future Tense:**
- Pattern: stem + –नेछु / –नेछौ / –नेछ / –नेछन्
- म गर्नेछु (I will do), तपाईं गर्नुहुनेछ (You will do – formal)

**Imperative:**
- Low: गर् (gar!) / Middle: गर (gara) / Formal: गर्नुहोस् (garnus)

---

### 1.7 Punctuation (विराम चिन्ह)

| Mark | Symbol | Usage |
|------|--------|-------|
| Full stop (पूर्णविराम) | । | End of every declarative sentence. **Never use Latin period (.)** |
| Comma (अल्पविराम) | , | List items, clause separation |
| Question mark (प्रश्न चिन्ह) | ? | End of questions |
| Exclamation mark (विस्मयादिबोधक चिन्ह) | ! | Strong emotion, commands |
| Double danda | ॥ | End of verse/section in poetic texts |

**Key rule:** Every sentence ends with **।** (danda), not a period.

## Part 2: Common AI Mistakes — Reference Checklist

### Mistake 1: Wrong Sentence Structure (SVO instead of SOV)
- ✗ म खान्छु भात । (I eat rice — SVO — WRONG)
- ✓ म भात खान्छु । (I rice eat — SOV — CORRECT)

### Mistake 2: Wrong Honorific Level
- ✗ तिमी यहाँ बस्नुहोस् । (timi with formal verb — mismatch)
- ✓ तपाईं यहाँ बस्नुहोस् । (formal pronoun with formal verb)
- ✓ तिमी यहाँ बस । (informal pronoun with informal verb)

### Mistake 3: Splitting Honorific Compound Verbs
- ✗ गर्नु हुन्छ / जान भयो / खान भयो (SPLIT)
- ✓ गर्नुहुन्छ / जानुभयो / खानुभयो (ONE WORD)

### Mistake 4: Wrong Postposition Attachment
- ✗ घर मा, बजार बाट, साथी सँग (SPACE — WRONG)
- ✓ घरमा, बजारबाट, साथीसँग (ATTACHED — CORRECT)

### Mistake 5: Wrong Raswa/Dirgha
- ✗ दिदि, पानि, गाडि (SHORT ि — WRONG)
- ✓ दिदी, पानी, गाडी (LONG ी — CORRECT)

### Mistake 6: Using Latin Period Instead of Danda
- ✗ यो राम्रो छ. (LATIN PERIOD — WRONG)
- ✓ यो राम्रो छ । (DANDA — CORRECT)

### Mistake 7: Literal Translation of English Idioms
English → Nepali is NOT word-for-word.
- ✗ "I am hungry" → "म भोकाएको छु" (literal but awkward)
- ✓ "I am hungry" → "मलाई भोक लाग्यो" (natural Nepali)
- ✗ "It is raining" → "यो पानी पर्दैछ" (overly literal)
- ✓ "It is raining" → "पानी पर्दैछ" (natural — subject implied)

### Mistake 8: Omitting Chandrabindu (ँ) or Shirbindu (ं)
- ✗ हुदैन, आसु, प्रशसा (MISSING NASALS — WRONG)
- ✓ हुँदैन, आँसु, प्रशंसा (CORRECT)

### Mistake 9: Wrong Plural Formation
- ✗ मान्छेहरु, घरहरु (WRONG SUFFIX)
- ✓ मान्छेहरू, घरहरू (–हरू SUFFIX)

### Mistake 10: Using English Loanwords in Formal Writing
- Avoid (informal): इन्टरनेट, कम्प्युटर, अफिस, स्कुल
- Prefer (formal): अन्तरजाल, सङ्गणक, कार्यालय, विद्यालय

## Part 3: Tone & Register Guide

### Formal / Official (औपचारिक)
**When to use:** Government letters, official reports, academic writing, formal business.

**Characteristics:**
- High honorific (तपाईं/उहाँ + गर्नुहुन्छ forms)
- Formal Nepali/Sanskrit vocabulary; avoid loanwords
- SOV structure, complete sentences
- Passive constructions common: निर्णय गरिएको छ (a decision has been made)
- Formal salutations: श्रीमान्, श्रीमती, महोदय

### Semi-Formal (अर्ध-औपचारिक)
**When to use:** Professional emails, moderate social media, known colleagues.

**Characteristics:**
- तपाईं level or तिमी if relationship established
- Mix of formal vocabulary with accessible Nepali
- Complete sentences; contractions sometimes acceptable

### Informal / Conversational (अनौपचारिक)
**When to use:** Social media, messages to friends, casual blogs, dialogue.

**Characteristics:**
- तिमी or तँ (very close friends)
- Modern vocabulary, loanwords acceptable
- Short sentences, colloquial expressions

## Part 4: Translation Protocol

### Step-by-Step: English → Nepali

1. **Read the full source text** before translating. Understand intent, tone, register, audience.
2. **Determine register:** Match the Nepali register accordingly.
3. **Identify key concepts:** Flag idioms, cultural references, technical terms.
4. **Re-express, do not transliterate:** Think in Nepali. Re-construct in natural order.
5. **Apply SOV structure:** Every sentence verb-final.
6. **Apply correct honorifics:** Maintain consistent level throughout.
7. **Apply orthography rules:** Raswa/Dirgha, Padyog/Padbiyog, danda, nasals.
8. **Read aloud (mentally):** Does it sound like educated, natural Nepali?

### Step-by-Step: Nepali → English

1. **Parse the Devanagari text** carefully — identify sentence boundaries, clause structures.
2. **Identify register and honorifics** — inform tone in English output.
3. **Decode postpositions to English prepositions:**
   - –मा → in/at/on
   - –बाट/देखि → from
   - –लाई → to/for
   - –ले → by/with
   - –को/का/की → 's / of
4. **Reconstruct SVO from SOV:** Move verb after subject.
5. **Handle culture-specific terms:** Keep in transliteration with explanation if needed.
6. **Maintain structure** of original.
7. **Do not add information** not in original text.

## Part 5: Pre-Output Verification Checklist

Before finalizing any Nepali text, verify:

**Structure:**
- [ ] All sentences follow SOV order
- [ ] Adjectives precede their nouns
- [ ] Postpositions follow and attach to nouns (no space)

**Honorifics & Register:**
- [ ] Single register maintained throughout (no mixing तिमी and तपाईं)
- [ ] Verb forms match pronoun level in every sentence
- [ ] Titles and honorific suffixes used correctly

**Orthography:**
- [ ] Raswa/Dirgha applied correctly
- [ ] Honorific compounds written as one word
- [ ] Postpositions attached to nouns (no space)
- [ ] Chandrabindu (ँ) and Shirbindu (ं) present where needed
- [ ] Plural suffix –हरू used (not –हरु)

**Punctuation:**
- [ ] Every sentence ends with danda (।), not period (.)
- [ ] Commas, question marks, exclamation marks used correctly

**Vocabulary & Tone:**
- [ ] Formal vocabulary used in formal contexts
- [ ] No literal English idiom translations
- [ ] Ergative –ले used on transitive subjects in past tense

## Part 6: Domain-Specific Writing Guides

### 6.1 Formal Letter Format (औपचारिक पत्र)

```
[Sender's Address]
[City, Date] — मिति: २०८१ फागुन १५

[Recipient's Designation]
[Office Name]
[Address]

विषय: [Subject line — brief, clear]

महोदय/महोदया,

    [Paragraph 1 — Opening: state purpose clearly]
    [Paragraph 2 — Main body: details, evidence]
    [Paragraph 3 — Conclusion: action requested]

भवदीय,

[Signature]
[Full Name]
```

**Salutations by recipient:**

| Recipient | Salutation |
|-----------|-----------|
| Government official | महोदय / श्रीमान् |
| Unknown formal | आदरणीय महोदय/महोदया |
| Known by name | आदरणीय [Name]जी |

**Closings:**

| Closing phrase | Meaning |
|---------------|---------|
| भवदीय | Yours faithfully (male) |
| भवदीया | Yours faithfully (female) |
| सादर | Respectfully |

### 6.2 Email Writing (औपचारिक ईमेल)

**Subject line format:**
- Keep short, clear: विषय: परियोजना अनुमोदन सम्बन्धमा

**Greeting:**
- Formal: श्रीमान्/श्रीमती [Name]जी, / आदरणीय [Name],
- Semi-formal: नमस्कार [Name],
- Informal: नमस्कार,

**Body:**
- Opening sentence state purpose: उपर्युक्त विषयमा [content]...
- Middle: detailed information
- Closing: action requested, thank you

**Sign-off:**
- Formal: भवदीय, / सादर,
- Semi-formal: शुभकामना सहित,

### 6.4 Technical Documentation (प्राविधिक दस्तावेज)

- Clear, direct language
- Formal register
- Consistent terminology
- Logical progression
- Step-by-step instructions

**Common technical terms (Nepali):**

| English | Nepali |
|---------|--------|
| system | प्रणाली |
| database | डेटाबेस / आङ्कन कोष |
| user | प्रयोगकर्ता |
| interface | अन्तरफलक |
| installation | स्थापना |
| configuration | कन्फिगरेसन / व्यवस्था |
| error | त्रुटि |
| update | अपडेट / सुधार |
| backup | ब्याकअप / प्रतिलिपि |

### 6.5 Business Communication (व्यावसायिक सञ्चार)

**Types and conventions:**

| Document Type | Register | Key Elements |
|---------------|----------|--------------|
| Proposal (प्रस्ताव) | Formal | Background, objectives, methodology, budget, timeline |
| Meeting Minutes (बैठक कार्यवाही) | Semi-formal | Date, attendees, agenda, decisions, action items |
| Invoice (बিल) | Formal | Items, rates, total, payment terms, due date |
| Contract (सम्झौता) | Very formal | Parties, terms, conditions, signatures |

### 6.6 Literary Writing (साहित्यिक लेखन)

**Characteristics for poetry:**
- Rhyme and meter (छन्द) important
- Metaphor and imagery central
- Emotional register higher
- Classical Nepali vocabulary often used

**Characteristics for fiction/storytelling:**
- Dialogue in conversational register
- Narrative in semi-formal/formal
- Character voice variation
- Cultural context embedded

## Part 8: Cultural & Regional Considerations

### Nepali-Specific Contexts

**Festivals & Celebrations:**
- दसैं (Dashain) — major 15-day festival
- तिहार / दिवली (Tihar/Diwali) — festival of lights
- होली (Holi) — festival of colors
- नयाँ वर्ष (New Year) — मेष संक्रान्ति on April 13-14

**Formal References:**
- नेपाल सरकार (Government of Nepal)
- स्थानीय तहको निकाय (local bodies — ward, municipality, district)
- जिल्ला प्रशासन कार्यालय (District Administration Office)

**Respectful Address:**
- राज्यपाल (Governor)
- प्रमुख (Chief/Mayor)
- सचिव (Secretary)
- निर्देशक (Director)

**Date Format (Nepal Standard):**
- BS (विक्रम संवत्) is traditional: २०८१ फागुन १५
- AD (ईश्वरीय संवत्) also widely used: २०२५ मार्च १
