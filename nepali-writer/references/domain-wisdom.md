# Domain Wisdom: Nepali Language Standards & Best Practices

Core principles and reference guide for Nepali writing, extracted from Nepal Academy standards and common AI mistakes.

---

## Part 1: Foundational Wisdoms

### 1. Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) Word Order is Fundamental
**Rule:** Nepali follows SOV structure, not SVO like English.  
**Wrong:** I coffee drink (SVO — English order)  
**Correct:** म कफी पिन्छु (I coffee drink — SOV order)  
**Why:** Verb must come at sentence end. This is the foundational structure of Nepali grammar.

---

### 2. The Three-Level Honorific System Rules Everything
**Levels:**
- **तँ** (most informal, used only with very close friends/family)
- **तिमी** (informal/semi-formal, used with peers)
- **तपाईं** (formal/respectful, used with elders, officials, strangers)

**Critical Rule:** Pronouns and verbs must match in honorific level.  
**Wrong:** तिमी गर्नुहोस् (informal pronoun + formal verb)  
**Correct:** तपाईं गर्नुहोस् (formal pronoun + formal verb)  
**Why:** Mixing honorifics sounds disrespectful or confusing.

---

### 3. Emotions & Sensations Use Dative, Not Nominative
**Grammar:** Emotions "happen to" the person, not "are" in the person.  
**Wrong:** म खुसी छु (I happy am — nominative)  
**Correct:** मलाई खुशी लाग्यो (happiness touched me — dative)  
**Apply to:** happy, sad, hungry, cold, afraid, excited, confused  
**Why:** Reflects how Nepali conceptualizes experiences (external event vs. internal state).

---

### 4. Postpositions Attach Without Spaces (Padyog)
**Rule:** Postpositions are written as one unit with the noun they modify.  
**Wrong:** घर मा (house + space + postposition)  
**Correct:** घरमा (house + postposition as one word)  
**Examples:**  
- टेबलमा (on the table)  
- घरबाट (from home)  
- मेरोसँग (with me)  
**Why:** Postpositions are grammatical morphemes, not separate words.

---

### 5. Raswa/Dirgha Vowel Length Distinguishes Meaning
**Concept:** Short vowels (raswa ु, ि) vs. long vowels (dirgha ू, ी) change word meaning.  
**Example pairs:**
| Short (Raswa) | Meaning | Long (Dirgha) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| दिदि | younger sister | दिदी | elder sister |
| पनि | also/too | पानी | water |
| कुरा | matter/thing | कुरा | (same, but emphasis varies) |

**Why:** Essential for spelling and meaning. Common AI mistake.

---

### 6. Danda (।) Punctuation, Not Latin Period
**Rule:** Nepali sentences end with दण्ड (।), not period (.).  
**Wrong:** यो राम्रो छ.  
**Correct:** यो राम्रो छ ।  
**Double danda (॥):** Used for major section breaks  
**Why:** Script consistency; shows native Nepali orthography.

## Part 2: Domain-Specific Wisdoms

### 7. Formal Writing: Use Complete Sentences & Formal Vocabulary
**Formal vocabulary alternatives:**
| Casual | Formal |
|--------|--------|
| अफिस | कार्यालय |
| प्रोजेक्ट | प्रकल्प |
| टीम | टोली or दल |
| सहायता | सहयोग |

**Structure:** Complete, complex sentences with formal transitional phrases (उपर्युक्त विषयमा, सादर निवेदन गर्दछु)  
**Why:** Shows respect and professionalism.

---

### 8. Technical Writing: Mix Native Nepali + Standard Technical Terms
**Strategy:**
- Use Nepali equivalents where clear: व्यवस्थापक (administrator), डेटा (data)
- Transliterate where no Nepali standard exists: अपडेट (update), इन्टरनेट (internet)
- Be consistent: Choose one term and stick to it throughout document

**Example:**
- प्रणाली (system) or सिस्टम (both acceptable; choose one and stick)
- अनुप्रयोग (application) or एप्लिकेसन (both acceptable; choose one and stick)

**Why:** Clarity for mixed audiences (both general and technical).

---

### 9. Social Media: Tone Trumps Grammar
**Rules:**
- Conversational register acceptable
- Code-mixing acceptable (कफी, पेस्ट्री)
- Short, punchy sentences over complex structures
- Emojis and hashtags are standard

**Constraint:** Still avoid gross grammar errors (honorific mismatches, wrong postposition spacing).  
**Why:** Authenticity and engagement matter more than formal perfection.

---

### 10. Literary Writing: Embrace Rich Vocabulary & Metaphor
**Characteristics:**
- Deeper honorific choices (तिमी vs तपाईं convey relationship nuance)
- Poetic metaphors and idioms
- Varied sentence structure for rhythm
- Formal, elevated vocabulary

**Reference:** Study classical Nepali poetry and contemporary literary works.  
**Why:** Literary register is distinct from formal/technical/social.

## Part 3: Common AI Mistakes to Avoid

### 11. Literal Translation Fails
**Problem:** Word-for-word translation sounds unnatural.  
**Example:**
- English: "I understand" (nominative verb)
- Wrong: "म अन्डरस्ट्यान्ड गर्छु" (I understand do)
- Correct: "मलाई बुझियो" (Understanding came to me)

**Solution:** Understand Nepali idioms and translate intent, not words.

---

### 12. Incorrect Verb Selection
**Problem:** Using English verbs transliterated instead of Nepali verbs.  
**Examples:**
- "Please sit" ≠ "कृपया सिट गर्नुहोस्" (sit do — calqued from English)
- "Please sit" = "कृपया बस्नुहोस्" (Nepali verb बस्नु)

**Affected verbs:** sit (बस्नु), stand (खड़ा हुनु), go (जानु), come (आउनु)  
**Solution:** Use native Nepali verbs, not English verbs with गर्नु.

---

### 13. Compound Verb Splitting (Padyog Error)
**Problem:** Separating honorific compounds into two words.  
**Wrong:** गर्नु हुन्छ (split into two words)  
**Correct:** गर्नुहुन्छ (one word — honorific compound)  
**Examples:**
- बस्नुहोस् (not: बस्नु होस्)
- गर्नुपर्छ (not: गर्नु पर्छ)
- जानुहुन्छ (not: जानु हुन्छ)

**Solution:** Write honorific compounds as one unit.

---

### 14. Subject-Implied Weather Verbs
**Problem:** Adding unnecessary "it" pronoun to weather verbs.  
**Wrong:** यो पानी पर्दैछ (it rain is-falling — overly explicit)  
**Correct:** पानी पर्दैछ (rain is-falling — subject implied)

**Solution:** Omit subject when it's "weather" or implied by context.

## Part 4: Register & Tone Guidelines

### 15. Register Selection: Match to Relationship & Context
**Formula:** Register = Relationship Closeness + Topic Formality  
**Application:**
- Stranger + official topic = Formal (तपाईं + गर्नुहोस्)
- Colleague + professional topic = Semi-formal (तपाईं/तिमी + balanced verbs)
- Friend + casual topic = Informal (तिमी + गर्)

**Error:** Using formal register with friends feels distant; informal with officials feels disrespectful.

---

### 16. Tone Preservation in Translation
**Rule:** Match the original tone when translating.  
**Example:** Informal English → Informal Nepali (not formal)  
**Tool:** Check register-appropriate vocabulary and verb forms.

**Why:** Mismatched tone confuses the message and relationship.

---

## Part 5: Cultural & Reference Wisdoms

### 17. Cultural Context Matters in Translation
**Examples:**
- Greetings differ by context (शुभ प्रभात = good morning, formal)
- Formal letters use specific formulas (आदरणीय + विषय + सादर निवेदन)
- Respectable address includes जी suffix (शर्माजी, not just शर्मा)

**Solution:** Know Nepali culture and conventions, not just grammar rules.

---

### 18. Vocabulary Consistency Within Documents
**Rule:** Choose one term and use it consistently throughout.  
**Example:** If you use "अपडेट" for "update", don't switch to "सुधार" halfway through.  
**Tool:** Create a terminology glossary for long documents (technical docs, translations).

---

### 19. Nepal Academy Standards are Authority
**Reference:** Nepal Academy (प्रज्ञा-प्रतिष्ठान) publishes:
- Nepali Brihat Shabdakosh (dictionary)
- Orthography rules (including Raswa/Dirgha conventions)
- Modern Nepali conventions (post-2015 Constitution standards)

**When uncertain:** Check Nepal Academy standards, not subjective preference.

---

### 20. Continuous Learning from Native Speakers
**Wisdom:** No rule system captures all nuance. Listen to/read native Nepali.  
**Sources:**
- Contemporary Nepali literature
- News articles and journalism
- Social media and blogs
- Conversations with native speakers

**Why:** Language evolves; patterns emerge from real usage.
